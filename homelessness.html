Affording a Home

Economic recessions and the unemployment rate were cited as major contributors to the rising number of people of the streets. High inflation, a weakening currency, and limited job opportunities pushed many individuals and families into financial instability, making it difficult to afford housing and basic needs. Internal migration from rural areas to urban centers in search of work also strained resources in major cities, where affordable housing was already scarce. These economic pressures created a vulnerable population that, when combined with the impact of the earthquake, significantly worsened Turkey's homelessness crisis.

The Earthquake of 2023

While Turkey has a smaller percentage of homelessness than the United States, recent events such as the earth quake in 2023 displaced nearly 16% of its population. Turkey went from having an estimated homeless population of 150,000 before the earthquake, to a now estimated 1.5 million people after the disaster, with at least 230,000 houses destroyed.
